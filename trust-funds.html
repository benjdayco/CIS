Description

The Afghanistan Infrastructure Trust Fund supports technical assistance and grants for infrastructure investments, including the construction of roads, railways, airports, energy infrastructure, and water management and irrigation facilitie

Purpose

To pool and deliver financing for infrastructure development in Afghanistan and to leverage resources through cofinancing with private sactor and other development partners..

Remarks

Qualified IC: Eligible projects that come within the preferences stated in any qualified IC will be financed first from the amounts contributed under such qualified IC. Any shortfall will be financed out of the commingled funds held in the joint pool of funds. All other eligible investment projects will also be financed out of the commingled funds. Should qualified ICs be available from more than one contributor for a project at the time of allocation, the project will be financed from the amounts contributed under such qualified IC in proportion to their respective contributions (R205-10 para. 25). A contributor to AITF may state a preference for its contribution to be used to support either one or more of the sectors eligible for financing from the proceeds of AITF. In such case, the IC will reflect such preference and any such IC shall be deemed to be a "qualified IC." ADB will use its best efforts to apply the contribution for such sectors, as agree with the contributor. In the event that a contributor's preference cannot be accommodated, ADB may allocate the contribution to other sectors with the agreement of the contributor (R205-10 para. 21).

Approving Authority The approving authority for AITF financing be the same as for the related ADB financing, except as otherwise provided under ADB's policy on additional financing to the extent applicable (R55-12.2.ii).
